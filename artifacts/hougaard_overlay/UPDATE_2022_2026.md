## Headline: bear-regime alignment edge **CONFIRMED** on independent window

The original 2021-2025 VAL run had a striking but small-sample finding: bear-regime aligned trades had PF 5.17 vs neutral 0.74 (n=88 vs 23). The pure-OOS 2022-2024 window — which is independent and explicitly bear-rich (2022 drawdown) — replicates this **even more dramatically**:

| Window | Bear-regime aligned | Bear-regime neutral | Δ PF |
|---|---|---|---|
| Original VAL 2021-2025 | n=88, PF 5.17, avg $+26 | n=23, PF 0.74, avg $-5 | +4.42 |
| **PURE_OOS 2022-2024** | **n=16, PF 28.14, avg $+139** | **n=9, PF 0.86, avg $+29** | **+27.27** |
| OOS 2026 | n=14, PF inf (100% WR), avg $+241 | n=25, PF 7.31, avg $+154 | +large |
| **Combined bear** | **n=118, alignment helps** | **n=57, alignment doesn't help** | (consistent) |

In every bear-regime sub-sample tested, aligned trades outperform neutral trades by a meaningful margin. The 2022-2024 window is critical because (a) it's pure OOS for iter12 and (b) it has the most actual bear-regime trading days. The replication strengthens the case considerably.

## But the gate-opener still doesn't work — falsified again

Control comparison on PURE_OOS 2022-2024 (the test that decides whether the bias adds incremental edge over just lowering the threshold):

| delta | aligned PF | opposed PF | neutral PF | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| −0.05 | 4.35 | 4.46 | **4.83** | Aligned WORST |
| −0.08 | 4.27 | 4.13 | **4.87** | Aligned 2nd-worst |
| −0.12 | 4.04 | 3.76 | **4.65** | Aligned 2nd-worst |

Same result as the original VAL window: when you lower the threshold, aligned marginal trades are NOT better than neutral marginal trades. **The gate-opener mechanism is firmly dead.**

## Why these two findings aren't a contradiction

It looks paradoxical but isn't:
- **At full confidence (prob ≥ 0.75) in bear regime**: aligned trades win much more often than neutral. Edge concentrated where the ML model already wants to fire.
- **At marginal confidence (prob in [0.63, 0.75])**: aligned and neutral are equivalent. Edge does NOT propagate to lower-confidence signals.

The bias signal works as a **confidence amplifier** — it predicts well *among already-strong signals* — but does NOT serve as a sub-threshold filter to find hidden winners.
